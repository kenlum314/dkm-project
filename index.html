Introduction

This project came about as the group members (David, Ken, and Manny; a.k.a. DKM) met and realized their fantastic backgrounds could really help a BCIT instructor redesign a course website he had been using. All members attended the same class where the instructor (Jeff Parker) taught them about web development using HTML5, CSS3, jQuery, and other related technology. However one day they were discussing with Jeff how out-of-date his course web site was, and Jeff, who was rather startled by the suggestion (but not insulted), quickly mentioned that he was planning on making their final assignment one that could potentially replace the current website. In fact, Jeff offered a monetary reward to any team of students who could create a better website that met ALL his criteria for improvement. This website details how Project DKM came about and chronicles the process by which the DKM group passed the course with flying colors, collected the prize money, and forever became a legend amongst BCIT-trained web developers.
